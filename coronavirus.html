Welcome to the F1 Coronavirus helper. Here you can find all the information on what has been happening, what is currently happening and what is going to happen with formula 1 during this coronavirus period.

When will the 2020 F1 season start?

CEO of F1 Chase Carey has said to us that the plan is to host the 1st race of the season at Austria for the Austrian Grand Prix, the weekend date of which is 3-5 July.

CEO of F1 Chase Carey commented: “We will publish our finalised calendar as soon as we possibly can,” he also added that: “We all want the world to return to the one we know and cherish, yet we recognise it must be done in the right and safest way. We look forward to doing our part by enabling our fans to once again safely share the excitement of Formula 1 with family, friends, and the broader community."

    As of right now no known F1 drivers have contracted the Coronavirus.

    What is Formula 1 doing to help?

    An incredible story has recently come out about "Project Pitlane", where multiple F1 teams mostly based in Milton Keynes in United Kingdom, have built multiple Ventilators to help patients with Coronavirus help with breathing if they have the virus. The NHS being benefitted a lot from this as they have ordered a huge amount of these Ventilators.
